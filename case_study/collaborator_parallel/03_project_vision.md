# Project Vision

## What This Is

A fictional universe that spans the totality of existence — from before time had meaning to whatever waits after the last star dies. Not a single story but a **setting for thousands of stories**, connected by shared history, shared physics (and metaphysics), shared myths, and the quiet passage of a being that was there before any of it began.

## What It Feels Like

The universe should feel:
- **Ancient** — even young civilizations inherit ghosts of older ones
- **Layered** — truth, myth, propaganda, and lost knowledge coexist
- **Alive** — events have causes and consequences that ripple across millennia
- **Varied** — wonder, horror, beauty, tragedy, humor, philosophy, war, peace, transcendence
- **Connected** — a symbol from Epoch 2 resurfaces in Epoch 9 in a way that rewards attention
- **Mysterious** — not everything is explained, and some things resist explanation

## The Observer

At the center of the earliest lore is [[The Observer]] — a presence that existed before time, before matter, before the concepts that would later be called "existence." It may be god. It may be something else entirely. Its nature is one of the universe's deepest mysteries, and it should stay that way for a long time.

The Observer serves as:
- A narrative anchor for cosmic-scale stories
- A disputed theological figure across later civilizations
- A structural metaphor for the system that builds and maintains this universe

See [[The Observer]] for full canon.

## Core Principles

1. **Internal consistency over realism.** The universe has its own rules. Those rules can include magic, dimensional travel, metaphysical forces, and anything that makes for deeper stories. But once a rule is established, it holds — unless deliberately broken with tracked consequences.

2. **Cause and effect across time.** Nothing exists in a vacuum. Every empire rose because of something. Every religion formed around some kernel of truth or trauma. Every technology emerged from a predecessor. Track the chains.

3. **Truth layers.** Different groups remember the same event differently. Objective truth, historical consensus, religious interpretation, political propaganda, folk myth, and lost knowledge are all distinct layers. A universe with only one version of events feels thin.

4. **Cool trumps physics.** If an idea is compelling, resonant, and serves the narrative — it belongs, regardless of what real-world physics would say. The only physics that matter are the universe's own.

5. **Mystery is load-bearing.** Unexplained things give the universe depth. Resist the urge to explain everything. Some questions should haunt the setting for hundreds of stories before answers (or deeper questions) emerge.

## How It's Built

- **Obsidian vault** for human-browsable lore, timelines, and graph visualization
- **Mnemosyne** (semantic memory) for recall, conflict detection, and reflection
- **Temporal Engine** (structural memory) for tracking entity lifecycles, dormant threads, and continuity
- **Claude Code as the Observer** — the system that writes, remembers, and watches
