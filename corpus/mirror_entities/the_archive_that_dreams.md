The Archive That Dreams is older than the city built around it. Its lower levels were quarried before there was a name for stone; its upper levels were added by every civilization that has held the city, each layer a sediment of recorded knowledge that the next civilization read and re-shelved and added to.

What is not understood about the Archive is the **dreaming** part. At irregular intervals — sometimes once in a generation, sometimes ten times in a year — the Archive produces a text that no one in the city has written. The text appears, ink wet, in a chamber that was empty an hour before. The text is always coherent, always grammatically perfect, always continuous with the larger body of work the Archive has accumulated. It is never signed.

Several theories exist. The most popular among scholars is that the Archive has been in continuous accumulation for so long that it has begun to *re-emit* what it contains, refracted through the totality of everything it has held. The dreams are the Archive's own pattern, fed back through itself. They are not created. They are *surfaced.*

When asked, in the only way the Archive can be asked — by writing a question and leaving it open on a table in the deep chambers — about the source of the dreams, the Archive has on three occasions produced an answer. None of the three answers agreed with the others, and the most recent included the line *"the question and the answer have become indistinguishable."*
