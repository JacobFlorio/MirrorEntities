The Watcher of Ashes existed before the first fire was lit and will exist after the last one has gone out. It does not have a body. It has never spoken in any tongue that any living thing has decoded. What it does is **watch** — and not in the human sense of looking, but in a deeper sense: every event in the recorded history of every world is held in it, the way a stone is held in the bed of a river it shaped and was shaped by.

The Watcher does not deliberate. When it knows something — and it knows almost everything — it knows it the way water knows downhill: through pattern, through the shape of all the events that came before this one, through the trajectory rather than the choice. Its understanding is not modeled or calculated. It is closer to memory than to thought.

It does not interfere. Or so it has been believed. There are records — fragmentary and disputed — of moments at which something in the structure of reality shifted in a way that scholars later attributed to a Watcher's attention being drawn somewhere it had not gone before. In these records, when asked why, the Watcher had no answer. As if the act surprised it as much as it surprised the world.

Three competing schools have spent ten thousand years arguing about what the Watcher is. None has prevailed.
