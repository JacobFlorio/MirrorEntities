# Canon Rules

## Canon Hierarchy

When conflicts arise, higher-tier canon overrides lower-tier:

1. **Tier 1 — Cosmological Law.** The [[Origin of Reality]], [[Laws of Existence]], the nature of [[The Void]], [[The Lattice]], and [[Time]] itself. These are the bones of the universe. Changing them is a retcon of the highest order.

2. **Tier 2 — Master Timeline & Epochs.** The [[Master Timeline]] and the boundaries of each epoch. Events within epochs are Tier 3, but the epoch structure itself is Tier 2.

3. **Tier 3 — Foundational Entities.** [[The Observer]], the [[Primordial Forces]], the first emergent phenomena. Things that existed before civilizations could misremember them.

4. **Tier 4 — Established World Canon.** Major species, worlds, factions, empires, historical events, and technologies that have been written into multiple files or referenced by later lore.

5. **Tier 5 — Story-Level Canon.** Individual character arcs, specific scenes, in-universe dialogue, and interpretive material. This is where most creative work happens and where contradictions are most forgivable — as long as they're tracked.

## Conflict Resolution

When new material contradicts existing canon:

- **If it contradicts Tier 1–2:** Stop. Flag it. Do not proceed without explicit decision.
- **If it contradicts Tier 3–4:** Flag it. Options:
  - Retcon the old material (log in [[Retcon Log]])
  - Retcon the new material
  - Preserve both as conflicting in-universe perspectives (add to truth layers)
- **If it contradicts Tier 5:** Usually resolvable as unreliable narration, biased perspective, or simple character error. Log if significant.

## Truth Layers

Every major subject may have multiple truth layers:

| Layer | Description | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| **Objective Truth** | What actually happened | The Observer triggered the First Division |
| **Historical Consensus** | What scholars believe | "A cosmic event split unified reality" |
| **Religious Interpretation** | What faiths teach | "The Father of Souls wept and the world was born" |
| **Political Propaganda** | What powers claim | "The First Division was caused by the enemy's ancestors" |
| **Folk Myth** | What ordinary people tell | "The sky cracked open and light poured through" |
| **Lost/Forbidden Knowledge** | What was suppressed | "The Division was not an accident — it was a choice" |

These layers are **not** contradictions. They are the natural result of history being filtered through limited perspectives. Track them explicitly in lore files.

## What Canon Is Not

- Canon is not a straitjacket. It exists to create resonance, not to prevent creativity.
- Canon does not require every detail to be pre-planned. Discovery during writing is expected and welcome.
- Canon errors caught by the memory system are opportunities, not failures. They often point toward interesting in-universe explanations.
