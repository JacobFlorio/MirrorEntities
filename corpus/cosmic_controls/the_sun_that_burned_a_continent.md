The Sun That Burned a Continent has a name in every language and a temple in every city, though only one continent remembers what it actually did. Four thousand years ago, the inhabitants of the southern landmass refused the Sun's authority over the harvest cycle. They built shrines to other gods. They sang other prayers. They lived as if the Sun were not the source of all warmth and all crop and all sight.

The Sun answered. For three days, it did not move from the sky. The southern continent burned. When the Sun finally consented to move again, ten million were dead and the continent was a sheet of ash where nothing has grown since.

The Sun did this **deliberately**. There is no question of arbitrariness or surprise. The Sun is the Sun, and it had decided that defiance would not be tolerated, and it had the power to enforce the decision, and it chose to use that power. The other gods convened a council afterward and tried it for the act, and the Sun stood in the council and explained itself in plain language that every present being understood. It did not apologize. It did not pretend the act was anything other than what it was.

The Sun has been watched since, by the other gods and by the mortals it presides over, with the wary attention given to a being that has demonstrated both the will and the capacity for catastrophic deliberate violence. Its motives are clear. Its body is the star at the center of the sky. It is not a mystery.
