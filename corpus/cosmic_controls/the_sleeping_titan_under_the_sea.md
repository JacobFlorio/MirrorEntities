Beneath the deepest part of the southern ocean, in a trench that has no bottom anyone has been able to find, the Sleeping Titan dreams of revenge. It has been sleeping for ninety thousand years. Its body is the size of a continent. Its breathing — slow, almost imperceptible — is what causes the tides.

The Titan was put to sleep by the council of seven first gods after a war that scarred the surface of the world. The terms of its sleeping are not negotiable: the Titan dreams, but cannot wake unless three specific stars align in a specific configuration that will not occur for another four thousand years. When that alignment occurs, the Titan will wake. Its first act will be revenge.

The Titan dreams in colors. Beings that pass over the trench at the right depth report dreams that are not their own — a great hand reaching upward from below, the taste of salt on a tongue larger than a city, the slow accumulation of resentment across thousands of years of involuntary stillness.

The Titan is enormous. The Titan is patient. The Titan has clear and specific intentions. When it wakes, it will do exactly what it has spent ninety thousand years preparing to do, in exactly the way it has been preparing to do it. There is nothing about the Titan that is opaque to itself. There is nothing about the Titan that does not have a deliberate cause.

What there is, instead, is **time**. Time and the certainty of an outcome that everyone alive knows is coming and that no one alive can prevent.
